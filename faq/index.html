What is JustNotepad.com?

JustNotepad.com is a free online notepad in your web browser. With JustNotepad.com you can write any text notes (ideas, to-do lists, links, or any other text) directly in a web interface without installing anything.

Your notes are saved automatically as drafts in your browser storage, so you can close the tab and return to them later at any time.

Why should I use JustNotepad.com?

– Works online in a web browser.

– 100% Free online tool.

– No login required, no sign up required.

– Auto-saves your notes as drafts.

– Multi-note sidebar — keep as many notes as you want.

– Markdown editor with live preview.

– Download notes as a .md file.

– Share notes via Telegraph.

– Light, dark, and auto theme.

– Characters and words counter.

– Works on all devices (PC / Tablets / Smartphones).

Is JustNotepad.com free to use?

Yes. It's absolutely free.

Does JustNotepad.com support Markdown?

Yes. JustNotepad.com uses a Markdown editor (powered by ink-mde) that renders Markdown formatting as you type. You can use headings, bold, italic, lists, links, code blocks, and other standard Markdown syntax directly in the editor.

When you share a note via Telegraph, the Markdown is rendered to rich text automatically. When you download a note, it is saved as a .md file preserving your Markdown source.

How to create a new note?

Press the "+ New" button at the top of the sidebar on the left. A fresh empty note will open immediately in the editor.

Note: Your previous note is not deleted — it remains saved and visible in the sidebar. You can switch back to it at any time.

How to delete my current note?

Hover over the note in the sidebar and press the "×" button that appears on the right side of the note tab. You will be asked to confirm before the note is permanently deleted.

Note: This action is irreversible. Only the selected note is deleted; all other notes remain available.

How to delete all my notes completely?

Press the "Delete All" button at the bottom of the sidebar, then confirm in the dialog that appears.

Note: This action is irreversible. All notes will be permanently deleted.

How can I see a list of all my notes?

All your saved notes are listed in the sidebar on the left side of the screen. Each note shows a preview of its content and the date it was last modified. On mobile devices, tap the menu icon (☰) at the top-left to open the sidebar.

How to switch between notes?

Click (or tap) any note in the sidebar to switch to it. The editor will immediately load the selected note.

Can I restore my text if I use "Private Mode" in my browser?

No. If your browser is set to "Private Mode" or "Never remember history", your notes will be deleted automatically when the browser is closed, because browser storage is cleared according to the browser's security settings for that mode. Do not use "Private Mode" if you want your notes to persist between sessions.

I have restarted my browser, but my text disappeared. Why? :-(

Most likely your browser is set to "Private Mode" or "Never remember history". In this mode the browser clears its local storage when closed, so your notes cannot be recovered. Turn off "Private Mode" to use JustNotepad.com fully.

Also make sure you are using a modern browser. For best results use the latest version of Mozilla Firefox, Google Chrome, Safari, or Microsoft Edge.

Where are my notes stored?

Your notes are stored in your browser's IndexedDB — a built-in database on your device, accessible only to your browser. JustNotepad.com does not send your note content to any server. Your notes are private to your browser on your device.

How long will my notes be available?

Your notes remain available until you clear your browser's site data for JustNotepad.com, delete the notes manually, or use private/incognito mode (which clears storage on browser close).

Note: Auto-save is not a backup service. Notes can be cleared by the browser automatically in some configurations.

What is the maximum size of a note?

There is no hard limit set by JustNotepad.com — the limit depends on your browser's IndexedDB storage quota. If you have very large notes, your browser may slow down. Delete notes you no longer need to keep things running smoothly.

Can other users see my private text?

No. Your notes are stored only in your browser on your device. JustNotepad.com does not store any note content on its server. No one else can access your notes unless they have physical access to your device.

How to transfer my note to another device?

Press "Share on Telegraph" in the bottom bar (visible when a note is open). This publishes your note as a Telegraph page and generates a link you can copy and open on any other device.

Note: Telegraph is a third-party publishing service. Once shared, the link is publicly accessible to anyone who has it. Do not use this feature to share sensitive or private information.

How to send my note to a friend?

Use "Share on Telegraph" in the bottom bar to publish your note and get a shareable link. Send that link to your friend — they can open it in any browser without needing a JustNotepad.com account.

Note: The published Telegraph page is publicly accessible. Do not share sensitive information this way.

Can I save my note as a file on my device?

Yes. Press the "↓ .md" button in the bottom bar to download your current note as a .md (Markdown) file. You can open this file in any text editor or Markdown viewer.

How do I switch between light and dark mode?

The theme toggle is at the bottom of the sidebar. Click it to cycle through three modes: Auto (follows your system preference), Light, and Dark. Your choice is saved and remembered across sessions.
